Common trader questions

The questions we get on the phone, answered.

17 years of pre-sale conversations has given us a solid list. If yours isn’t here, call us or use the booking form above.

Do I really need a specialist trading computer?

Honest answer: if you’re running one or two screens on MT4, a decent off-the-shelf PC will get you going. The case for a specialist machine kicks in when you start hitting real trading workloads — five or more screens, multiple platforms simultaneously, backtesting overnight, Bloomberg, or when any millisecond of chart lag starts costing you.

If we think you don’t need us, we’ll tell you. That’s why our Trustpilot score looks like it does.

Why not just buy a gaming PC? They have fast CPUs and GPUs.

Gaming PCs are built for one heavy foreground task. Trading is many concurrent medium-weight tasks — charting, data feeds, platform event loops — all demanding consistent low-latency throughput rather than peak frame-rate spikes. The parts, the cooling, the RAM config and the driver stack differ.

It’s also why gaming GPUs often cause weird trading-platform artefacts. Our builds use professional-grade GPUs specifically qualified against the major platforms.

What’s the real difference between the Trader PC and the Trader Pro?

Trader PC is the right call for traders running MT4/MT5, TradingView, MultiCharts, or NinjaTrader without heavy overnight strategy analysis — up to about six 1440p screens. Single-thread performance is the priority and the Trader PC is excellent there.

Trader Pro earns its price when you’re running strategy analysers overnight, working in NinjaTrader 8 or TradeStation with multi-instrument backtests, using Bloomberg, or going beyond six screens. The extra cores, extra RAM and extra screen headroom are all real working gains.

The quiz above will make the call for you. Or phone 0330 223 66 55 and Darren will.

How long does it take to build and deliver?

Standard configurations ship in 5–7 working days from order. Custom specs (bigger RAM, specific GPU, specific storage layout) add another 2–4 working days. We always tell you up front; we don’t take your money and then email you about delays.

What’s the 5-year cover actually cover?

Parts and labour on any hardware fault, for five years from delivery. UK collection or on-site at our discretion. That’s two years longer than most US competitors and three years longer than Scan’s equivalent.

Lifetime support means phone and email help with software, OS, platform setup, reinstalls — for the lifetime of the machine, without a clock running on a support contract. Still helping customers who bought in 2013.

Can I upgrade later?

Yes — every machine is built with upgrade paths in mind. Extra RAM, additional storage, GPU upgrade for more screens, even CPU upgrades within the same generation. We’ll talk you through what’s worth upgrading later vs. specifying now.

Do you deliver outside the UK?

We ship across the UK as standard (free on bundles). Europe and the rest of the world by quote — please email us with your destination and we’ll come back with a delivery quote and local VAT/import guidance.

I’m worried about the price. Is it really worth the premium?

Fair question. Here’s the honest take: if you’re a hobbyist trading part-time with a couple of screens, the premium probably isn’t justified for you — a well-chosen Scan or CCL build will do the job.

If trading is how you make your living, the difference between a machine that handles your workload and one that almost does is a real cost. A chart that lags when you’re scaling out of a position costs more than the price difference between our Trader Pro and a generic build.

And the lifetime support is a genuine long-term saving for anyone who doesn’t enjoy troubleshooting their own PC at 6:30am on an NFP Friday.
