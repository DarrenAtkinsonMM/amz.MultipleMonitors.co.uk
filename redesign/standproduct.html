How it mounts

Clamps to your desk. No drilling, no damage.

The Quad Square attaches to the back edge of your desk with a heavy-duty steel C-clamp — the same clamp we’ve shipped on every Synergy Stand since 2016. Fits desks from 15 mm thin veneer up to 55 mm solid oak, and comes off as easily as it goes on.

If your desk has a rear cable grommet, you can route the column through it for a cleaner look. The stand needs a desk edge to clamp to — it’s not designed to stand freely on the floor or on a wall.

Cable management

Cables hidden. Not “tucked”.

The central column is hollow. Power, DisplayPort, HDMI and USB runs go in at the base and come out at each mount — out of sight, out of your cat’s way, out of the way of vacuuming.

Every stand ships with cable management clips for the lengths that run outside the column (mainly between the arm and the monitor). On a clean install you see the stand, you see the screens, and nothing else.

Quad Square questions

The six questions we’re asked most often.

Specific to this stand — not generic stand-shop answers. Got a question not listed? Call us on 0330 223 66 55.

Will my 27″ or 28″ curved monitors fit?

Yes — up to 28″ flat or curved is supported on every mount. The arms have enough reach and the pivot has enough angle that even 28″ curved panels can be angled into a gentle curve across the top row and the bottom row.

The limit you’ll hit first is usually weight, not size: 8 kg per mount is generous for any 24″–27″ panel but a handful of 28″ curved models approach that. If in doubt, tell us the make & model when you order and we’ll confirm before we ship.

Can I upgrade to five, six or eight screens later?

Yes. The Quad Square uses the exact same central column and base as our five-, six- and eight-monitor stands. When you’re ready to grow, you buy arms and VESA plates — not a whole new stand.

Typical upgrade prices: +£70 for a pair of arms (takes you to six screens); +£140 for four arms (takes you to eight). We keep these parts in stock year-round. See the full range for exactly what each configuration looks like.

Desk clamp or grommet mount — which should I pick?

The Quad Square ships with a desk clamp as standard. It fits desks with a back edge between 15 mm and 55 mm thick — which is the vast majority of office and home desks.

If your desk has a rear cable grommet and you want the column to pass through it, ask for the grommet mount option at no extra cost. If your desk is against a wall and the clamp won’t fit, a freestanding base plate is available as a paid option (+£35) — mention it when you order.

How does the cable management work?

The central column is hollow. Power, DisplayPort, HDMI and USB cables go in at the base (or at the top, either works), run inside the column, and exit at each arm through a slot near the VESA plate.

The short runs between the column and each monitor are held in place with included clips. In practice, on a clean install, all you see is the stand and the screens — no dangling cables, no zip ties, no mess.

How long does assembly take and what tools do I need?

Most customers build a Quad Square on their own in 30–45 minutes. Two people can do it in 20. Nothing to drill, nothing to fix to the wall. Every tool you need (two Allen keys and a small spanner) ships in the box.

If you’d rather watch than read the instructions, the Synergy Stand assembly videos walk through it step-by-step. You can also download the printable assembly PDF.

What’s the warranty if something goes wrong?

Lifetime on every steel part. If an arm, a clamp, a VESA plate, or the column itself ever fails or bends, we replace it free, forever. Fixings (nuts, bolts, plastic clips) are covered for five years.

In 9 years of selling Synergy Stands we’ve seen a total of 11 warranty claims, mostly minor (replacement clips, re-shipped VESA plates). It’s a simple system of steel parts — not much goes wrong.
